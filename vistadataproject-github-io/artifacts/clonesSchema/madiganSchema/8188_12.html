description
This file contains the Systematized Nomenclature of Medicine (SNOMED) Clinical Terms (CT). SNOMED CT is a comprehensive and precise clinical reference terminology that provides clinical content for clinical documentation and reporting. The standardized clinical terminology enables the sharing of comparable data worldwide, across medical specialties, and sites of care. This is a Class 1 file, which cannot be edited by the site.

.02concept status0;2POINTER8188.13This field indicates the status (e.g., Current, Limited, Inactive) of a Concept. A "current" status indicates that the Concept is in current use and is considered active. A "limited" status indicates that the Concept is of limited clinical value as it is based on a classification concept or an administrative definition. Concepts with this status are still valid for current use and are considered active. An "inactive" status (such as Retired, Duplicate, Obsolete) indicates that the Concept is inactive and not for use.

.04primitive flag0;4SET OF CODES0:FULLY DEFINED
1:PRIMITIVE
This field indicates whether a SNOMED CT is Primitive or Fully defined by its current set of Defining characteristics. A Concept may be Primitive because (1) its only stated Defining characteristics are "is a" subtype relationship or (2) an aspect of its meaning is not fully expressed by existing Relationships. If a Concept is Primitive, its place in the subtype hierarchy may be know but it cannot be checked for equivalence with another Concept expressed or Post-coordinated expression. Per the source documentation, "A Concept is 'Fully defined" if its defining characteristics are sufficient to differentiate a concept relative to its immediate supertype(s). A Concept which is not "Fully defined" is Primitive. For example, if the Concept "Red car" is defined as [is a=car] + [color=red] this is "Fully defined" but the same definition applied to the Concept "Red sports car" is Primitive."
