.01hic0;1FREE TEXTH The Hierarchical Ingredient Code (HIC). Each HIC identifies an active ingredient of a particular drug product (identified by NDC). The HIC is a very special field, developed by Joseph L. Hirschmann, Pharm. D., retaining some very important relationships between the ingredient and its intended therapeutic use. The HIC is a six byte, alpha-numeric field utilizing the following format: 1st position->alphabetic Position format Description 1 Alpha Identifies Organ System 2 Numeric Positions 1-2 Identifies Pharmacological Class 3 Alpha Positions 1-3 Identifies Therapeutic Class* 4 Alpha Positions 1-6 identifies Ingredient 5 Alpha/Space Positions 5-6 identifies salt/ester 6 Alpha/Space Positions 5-6 identifies salt/ester The fifth and sixth positions are either both alphabetic or both blank. All six positions uniquely identify an ingredient. The inherent structure of the HIC provides additional information about the ingredient: Typically the first four characters of the HIC uniquely identify an ingredient with the last two characters containing blanks. When an ingredient comes in more than one salt form, the 5th and 6th positions identify the salt/ester. Examples are hydrochloride (HC), sodium (NA), sulfate (SU) or phosphate (PH). When a salt/ester code is applicable, all six positions of the HIC are required to identify the ingredient, with the first four identifying the base ingredient. For instance: "H3AH " = Codeine "H3AHPH" = Codeine Phosphate When an ingredient has one and only one salt form, the salt ester code is left blank, the first four characters identifying the complete ingredient. Procainamide Hydrochloride is an example of this, where the HIC is "A2AB ". The significance of the salt ester portion of the HIC is that a fourth relationship, between base ingredient and its salt and/or ester forms, is maintained. The HIC maintains the relationship of the ingredient to its intended therapeutic class (First DataBank's Specific Therapeutic Class - GC3, alias HIC3). Further, the ingredient also identifies the pharmacological class and organ system targeted by the intended use of the drug product.
