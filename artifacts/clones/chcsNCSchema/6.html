description
The PROVIDER file contains personal and professional data about the people who provide patient care services at this site. These include not only physicians, but also, for example, dentists, nurses, therapists, pharmacists, and technicians. This file also contains QA data concerning the provider's professional credentials. The Master File Primary Key (Unique ID) for this file: IEN\NAME(.01)\99STF

8613circuit rider9;9BOOLEAN1:YES
0:NO
'YES' entry indicates that the provider is functioning as a "Circuit-Rider". This is the case if the provider who is reading the exam has a borrowed labor agreement at this facility and the facility has a separate distinct Group ID than the provider's home reporting division. When a provider who is a Circuit-Rider with multiple reporting divisions defined accesses the Radiology Reporting menu (menu path RAD>ER), they will receive a prompt to define their current workload reporting division. 'NO' entry indicates that the provider is not functioning as a "Circuit-Rider". When a provider who is not a Circuit-Rider accesses the Radiology reporting menu (menu path RAD> ER), they will have only one reporting division defined for their profile. The term "Circuit-Rider" is used to distinguish the Radiologist that has a borrowed labor agreement with a Medical Treatment Facility on the same platform as their Home Reporting Division. The critical determinant is which Reporting Division the workload should be attributed to. Note: A radiologist is not designated a Circuit-Rider just because he or she performs interpretations for other divisions on the same CHCS regardless of Group ID; a Circuit-Rider MUST have a borrowed labor agreement with the other MTF if their workload is going to be credited to that MTF. If you are unsure whether your radiologist is a true Circuit-Rider please contact the Resource Management Office for confirmation.
