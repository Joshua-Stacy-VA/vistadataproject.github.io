.01drug0;1POINTER50BEnter a drug name, synonym, or therapeutic classification. The system returns the first picklist of entries matching your input that are already attached to your current ATC device. The entries in this picklist are identified with the letters "ATC" in parentheses to right of the drug name. This denotes those drug entries that already exist under your current ATC device. After selecting the ATC drug, the system allows you to edit or delete the ATC drug entry. If you press or F10 to reject the first picklist, the system displays a second picklist of candidates that do not include any of the drugs already attached your current ATC device. This list of drugs will not include the letters "ATC" in parentheses after the drug name. After you select a drug, you can add the drug to the ATC drug list. The system does not allow duplicate drug entries. If your input results in an empty first picklist, the system will only display the second picklist. If nothing matches your input, no list will be generated.
.02mnemonic(+)0;2FREE TEXTAMNEEnter a short, unique name or synonym associated with this medication. For example, the mnemonic for the drug ACETAMINOPHEN--PO 325MG could be defined as ACE325PO. This mnemonic has to be identical for the same drug on the ATC PC system. The mnemonics link the CHCS ATC drug list to the ATC PC drug list. All the mnemonics associated with an ATC device must be unique.
.03canister status0;3SET OF CODES1:**DISABLED**
ACANEnter a one (1) to disable a drug's ATC canister. Once disabled, the drug will be treated as a non-ATC drug on all outputs: the cartlist, the updated cartlist and the worklist. NO medication orders for this drug will be sent to the ATC system. This feature should be used whenever a canister will be temporarily out of service. To enable the drug again, delete the entry from the canister field.
