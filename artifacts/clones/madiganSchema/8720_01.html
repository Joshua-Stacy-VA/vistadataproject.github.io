.01sector number(+)0;1NUMERICThis is the number of the current sector for which you are defining physical dimensions. They should be sequentially numbered from left-to-right and top-to-bottom.
.02horizontal starting point(+)0;2NUMERIC This is the horizontal, or 'X', coordinate of the upper left corner of the sector. The value must range between zero and the label width. .03vertical starting point(+)0;3NUMERIC This is the vertical, or 'Y', coordinate of the upper left corner of the sector. The value must range between zero and the label height. Be careful not to place a sector within the space of another sector. Defining adjacent sectors requires at least one millionth of an inch (.000001") of space between each sector.
.04sector width(+)0;4NUMERIC Enter the width, in inches, of the sector being defined. The sector width must be less than or equal to the distance between the horizontal starting point and the label width. This value specifies the size of the sector from left to right, and is used to figure out if the sector spatially overlaps other already defined sectors.
.05sector height(+)0;5NUMERIC Enter the height, in inches, of the sector being defined. The sector height must be less than or equal to the distance between the vertical starting point and the label height. This value specifies the size of the sector from top to bottom, and is used to figure out if the sector spatially overlaps other alrady defined sectors.
