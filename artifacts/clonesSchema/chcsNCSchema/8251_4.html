AHFSDESCTBL - RAHFSD0 The American Hospital Formulary Service (AHFS) Description Table associates the AHFS number to a 40 byte description. The AHFS code is a foreign key attribute of the GCNSEQNOTBL. The Internal entry number (D0) is the numeric American Hospital Formulary Service (AHFS) classification system with leading zeros removed. If AHFS is zero 999999 is assigned.

.01ahfs(+)0;1NUMERICThe Therapeutic Class, AHFS identifies the pharmacologic therapeutic category of the drug product according to the American Hospital Formulary Service (AHFS) classification system. An AHFS# has been assigned for each record included in NDDF* whether or not the drug product is in the AHFS. For many drug products, particularly combination products, more than one AHFS code is possible. The selection of the included AHFS# is determined by consultation with the staff of the AHFS. Note A: Implied colon between positions 2 and 3. Implied period between positions 4 and 5. Note B: Conventionally, an AHFS classification number is printed with a colon between the second and third digits and a period between the fourth and fifth digits (e.g. 20:12.12). A leading zero is printed (e.g. 08:12.28 instead of 8:12.28). The fifth and sixth digits are generally omitted if zeros (e.g. 88:28). In NDDF* records, the colon and period are not included but all digits are carried, e.g. 8:04 becomes 080400 in NDDF*). Note C: The AHFS classification system is copyrighted by the American Society of Hospital Pharmacists and is used in NDDF* under a license agreement between AHFS and FDB. Note D: A default value of 999999 is used when a record has not been classified under the AHFS system. *NDDF is a Trade Mark of the Hearst Corporation
