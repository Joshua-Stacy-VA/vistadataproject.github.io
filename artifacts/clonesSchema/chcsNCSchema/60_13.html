.01accession area0;1POINTER68BEnter the appropriate accession area(s) for performing this test. If more than one accession area is entered, a selection will be made by the system based on test priority, the user's work element and division, and availability of the accession area. In the event that two areas meet the same selection criteria, the user will be prompted to select an accession area during log in. Should this case occur when logging in accessions on a collection list, the accessions will not be logged in and will have to be logged in separately instead. If the new test is a panel, all tests on the panel will be assigned to the selected accession area. If the test is a panel and the accession area is left blank, the individual tests on the panel are accessioned. If the tests on the panel are from two different accession areas, two accession numbers are generated. The subscript of the accession area must match the subscript defined for the test. All other accession areas with a different subscript will be screened and be unavailable for entry.
.02copath case type0;2FREE TEXTEnter a CoPath Case Type 1-20 characters in length. To enter more than one CoPath Case Type, separate the entries by a comma (ex: CO,R,S). This field contains the name of CoPath Case Types associated with the CoPath Prefix entered. CoPath Prefix and Case Types are configured by CoMed and can be found in the accessioning module for each CoPath department (Surgical Pathology, Cytology, and Autopsy). Entries will be used for mapping to CHCS accession areas and anatomic pathology lab tests and must be typed exactly as displayed on CoPath. Entries can be added, deleted, or edited.
